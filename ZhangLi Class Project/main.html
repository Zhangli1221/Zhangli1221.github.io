International events

Xinhua news agency, Geneva, November 30 (reporter Liu Qu) December 1 is World AIDS Day. The United Nations Programme on HIV / AIDS recently released a report saying that due to the impact of the new epidemic, global investment and action in the field of AIDS prevention and control is insufficient, which may lead to a large increase in the number of new HIV infections and deaths in the next two years.

The report estimates that in the next two years, the number of new HIV infections in the world may increase by 123000 to 293000, and the number of AIDS related deaths may increase by 69000 to 148000. Data show that in 2019, 1.7 million new HIV infected people and 690000 new HIV related deaths will be caused by AIDS. At present, there are 38 million HIV carriers in the world, and more than 12 million of them are waiting for treatment.

In the face of the continuous spread of the new HIV / AIDS epidemic, the executive director of the United Nations Programme on HIV / AIDS, Winnie baianima, called for global solidarity to learn from the mistakes of the past in dealing with HIV / AIDS. "No country can overcome these pandemics on its own. Only global unity, shared responsibility, and a response that does not leave anyone behind can we overcome such a huge challenge. "
